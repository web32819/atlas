Incredible India Jodhpur, Udaipur & Indore

LOCATION Jodhpur, Udaipur & Indore
DATES 20 Mar - 28 Mar'27
FROM 100

India has been celebrated for centuries for its colour, culture, and cuisine, but this trip goes beyond the well-worn tourist trail to reveal a side of the country that most travellers from the UK have never encountered. Spanning four of India's most captivating destinations, the dramatic blue-washed majesty of Jodhpur, the regal lakeside grandeur of Udaipur, the sacred ancient city of Ujjain, and the vibrant, famously clean streets of Indore, this journey is built around four extraordinary pillars: remarkable food, immersive cultural discovery, spiritual awakening, and one of the most spectacular festival experiences on earth.

Explore Mehrangarh Fort, one of India's most imposing fortresses, rising 120 metres above Jodhpur's iconic blue city. Drift across Lake Pichola at sunset toward Udaipur's shimmering lake palaces. Stand before the Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga in Ujjain, one of the 12 most sacred shrines in Hinduism and a place of pilgrimage for thousands of years, yet almost entirely unknown to Western visitors.

Learn to cook three classic dishes in a chef-led workshop, then sit down to a home-cooked dinner with a local Indian family. Graze through the legendary street food corridors of Sarafa Bazaar late into the night, and spend an evening immersed in the folk culture of a recreated Rajasthani village.

And then witness Holi and the Gair, Indore's thunderous Rang Panchami procession, where half a million people fill the streets in an explosion of colour, music, and dhol drums, a UNESCO-recognised spectacle that has no equivalent anywhere in the world.

Throughout, you'll stay at handpicked luxury properties in each city, from a heritage palace in Jodhpur to a lakeside retreat in Udaipur and a refined city hotel in Indore, ensuring every night is as memorable as the days that lead up to it.

Four cities. Eight nights. A lifetime of stories. This is India most travellers from the UK will never experience, and that is precisely the point.

Day by day

Itinerary

20 MAR

Arrival in Jodhpur

20 MAR
  • Arrive in Jodhpur and check in to Ajit Bhawan; freshen up and take in the extraordinary sight of the blue city stretching out beneath the towering walls of Mehrangarh Fort.
  • Evening walk through the narrow lanes of the old city, the famous blue-washed houses of Brahmpuri, the bustling Clock Tower market, and the sounds and smells of a Rajasthani evening in full flow.
  • Welcome dinner with your Atlas Experience representative, who will introduce the group and walk through the journey ahead.
21 MAR

Jodhpur: Mehrangarh Fort + Blue City

21 MAR
  • Morning visit to Mehrangarh Fort, one of the largest and most magnificently preserved forts in India, rising dramatically above the city with sweeping panoramic views across the blue rooftops below; explore its ornate palaces, museums, and ancient ramparts
  • Afternoon exploration of the blue city on foot, wander through the Ghanta Ghar bazaar, browse spices, textiles and handicrafts, and visit the serene Jaswant Thada memorial with its delicate white marble lattice work
  • Evening rooftop dinner overlooking the illuminated fort, one of the great views in Rajasthan and a perfect close to the day.
22 MAR

Travel to Udaipur + Holika Dahan

22 MAR
  • Morning at leisure in Jodhpur before boarding your private coach for the scenic 2.5-hour journey through the Rajasthani landscape to Udaipur; check in to the Trident hotel on the banks of Lake Pichola.
  • Afternoon boat ride across Lake Pichola to Jagmandir Island Palace, followed by a stroll through the old city lanes and a visit to the City Palace as the sun sets over the Aravalli hills.
  • In the Evening, witness Holika Dahan, the ceremonial bonfire marking the eve of Holi, observed with rituals, prayers, and community gathering around the flames; an intimate and deeply moving cultural moment that most visitors to India never experience.
23 MAR

Holi in Udaipur + Travel to Indore

23 MAR
  • Celebrate Holi morning in Udaipur, join locals in the streets for colour, music, and pure festivity; an explosion of joy that transforms even the most reserved traveller into a willing participant.
  • Board your private coach mid-morning for the scenic 5-hour journey to Indore, arriving in the evening to check in at The Park Indore.
  • Dinner at a local family home in Indore, a rare and warmly hosted experience that opens a window into everyday Indian life, food, and hospitality that no restaurant can replicate.
24 MAR

Rajwada + Cooking Workshop + Sarafa Bazaar

24 MAR
  • Morning visit to Rajwada Palace, the historic heart of Indore, built under the Holkar dynasty and still the civic and cultural soul of the city; explore the surrounding old bazaars and soak up the everyday rhythm of Indore life.
  • Afternoon chef-led cooking workshop: learn three iconic Indori dishes, poha (the city's legendary breakfast), shrikhand, and garadu (a beloved street snack unique to this region), plus one additional dish of your choice, in a hands-on, immersive session.
  • Late night food trail through Sarafa Bazaar, Indore's extraordinary jewellery market that transforms after dark into one of India's most celebrated street food destinations; finish the evening with a cup of late-night chai, a true Indore institution.
25 MAR

Indore at Leisure + Nakhrali Dhani

25 MAR
  • Morning and early afternoon at leisure to explore Indore's vibrant streets, browse the Cloth Market, or revisit favourite corners of the city at your own pace, a welcome breathing space after a few full days.
  • Late afternoon transfer to Nakhrali Dhani, a Rajasthani-themed village resort just outside the city, for an immersive evening of folk culture, traditional Kalbeliya and Ghoomar dance performances, puppet shows, and camel or horse cart rides set within a recreated village.
  • Dinner served in the traditional manuhar style, a generous, warmly hosted Rajasthani thali eaten the local way, rounding off a colourful and playful evening unlike anything else on the trip.
26 MAR

Ujjain: Mahakaleshwar Temple

26 MAR
  • Full day excursion to Ujjain, one of the seven sacred cities of Hinduism and home to the Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga, among the most revered of all 12 Jyotirlinga shrines; a place of uninterrupted pilgrimage for over 2,000 years and one of the most spiritually charged sites in all of India.
  • Explore the ancient ghats along the Shipra River, visit the Harsiddhi Temple, and walk the sacred lanes of a city that has barely changed in centuries, a profound contrast to the modern energy of Indore just 55km away.
  • Return to Indore in the evening; quiet dinner and early night ahead of tomorrow's main event.
27 MAR

The Gair: Rang Panchami Procession + Farewell Dinner

27 MAR
  • Witness the Gair, Indore's legendary Rang Panchami procession, one of the most extraordinary spectacles in Asia; hundreds of thousands of people gather at Rajwada Palace as the streets erupt in gulal, dhol drums, dance, and colour in a UNESCO-recognised celebration with roots stretching back to the Holkar royal dynasty.
  • The procession winds through the city in a wave of music and sheer euphoria; immerse yourself fully, dress to get drenched in colour, and surrender to one of the great human celebrations on earth.
  • Return to the hotel to freshen up before a group farewell dinner, celebrating the food, the festivals, the friendships, and everything India has given you across this unforgettable week.
28 MAR

Departure

28 MAR
  • Check out of The Park Indore and transfer to the airport, carrying home the colours, flavours, and memories of an India most travellers never get to see. This is India at its most authentic, vibrant, and surprising. A journey through places, traditions, and celebrations that remain hidden from most visitors, yet unforgettable to those fortunate enough to experience them.
